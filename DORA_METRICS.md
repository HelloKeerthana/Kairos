## 📊 DORA Metrics

<img src="https://www.pullflow.com/images/blog/DORAmetrics.png" alt="DORA Metrics" width="700"/>

DORA (DevOps Research and Assessment) metrics are four key engineering performance indicators used to measure software delivery speed, stability, and team effectiveness.

### 🟢 Deployment Frequency
**What it measures:** How often a team successfully deploys code to production.

**Why it matters:** High-performing engineering teams release changes frequently, allowing faster delivery of features, improvements, and bug fixes.

---

### 🟡 Lead Time for Changes
**What it measures:** The time taken for a code change to move from commit to production deployment.

**Why it matters:** Lower lead time indicates that teams can quickly transform ideas and fixes into value for users.

---

### 🔴 Change Failure Rate
**What it measures:** The percentage of deployments that result in failures, bugs, rollbacks, or production issues.

**Why it matters:** It helps evaluate deployment quality and shows whether teams can deliver changes without compromising reliability.

---

### 🔵 Failed Deployment Recovery Time (MTTR)
**What it measures:** The time required to recover from a failed deployment or restore normal service.

**Why it matters:** Faster recovery demonstrates system resilience and the team's ability to handle production failures effectively.
